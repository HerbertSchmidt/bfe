# Warehousing Language Training - Scenarios

## Scenario 1
**Category:** receiving
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** A delivery truck arrives with 12 pallets of canned goods. The driver hands you a delivery note but says two pallets are missing from the shipment. What do you do and what do you tell the driver?

## Scenario 2
**Category:** storage
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** You need to store 50 boxes of printer paper in the warehouse. The storage area has both floor space and pallet racking available. Which storage method do you choose and why?

## Scenario 3
**Category:** order_picking
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** You receive a pick list for 15 different items from various locations in the warehouse. Some items are on high shelves and some are heavy. Explain your picking strategy to ensure efficiency and safety.

## Scenario 4
**Category:** receiving
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** A shipment of electronic devices arrives but the packaging is damaged and shows signs of water exposure. You need to inspect the goods and document the situation. What steps do you take?

## Scenario 5
**Category:** packing_shipping
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** An urgent customer order needs to be shipped today before 4 PM. It contains fragile glassware and must travel 300 kilometers by truck. Describe what is important in preparing this shipment.

## Scenario 6
**Category:** storage
**Difficulty:** advanced
**Situation:** The warehouse manager asks you to reorganize a storage zone because the current layout causes too much travel time during picking. What factors do you consider?

## Scenario 7
**Category:** cross_docking
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** Two trucks arrive simultaneously with goods that need to be unloaded. How do you organize this, which steps do you take?

## Scenario 8
**Category:** order_picking
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** Your handheld scanner shows that an item's location has zero stock, but a customer order requires this item. What do you do to resolve this situation?

## Scenario 9
**Category:** traffic_management
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** You must choose between road transport and rail transport for shipping 20 pallets of textiles to a customer 500 kilometers away. The customer needs delivery within 2 days. Which mode do you recommend and why?

## Scenario 10
**Category:** inventory_management
**Difficulty:** advanced
**Situation:** The inventory system shows 150 units of a product, but during a physical count you find only 142 units. Your supervisor asks you to investigate. What could be the reasons?

## Scenario 11
**Category:** receiving
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** A supplier calls to inform you that their delivery will be 3 hours late. You have three other deliveries scheduled for today. How do you handle this situation?

## Scenario 12
**Category:** packing_shipping
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** A customer calls saying their shipment arrived with incorrect items. You need to arrange a return and send the correct items urgently. Explain the process you follow.

## Scenario 13
**Category:** storage
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** You discover that chemicals and food products are stored in the same warehouse zone. Is there a problem, explain your viewpoint.

## Scenario 14
**Category:** order_picking
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** You are being transferred to order picking. Explain your job, what do you do?

## Scenario 15
**Category:** traffic_management
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** A customer needs an emergency delivery by tomorrow morning. Normal transport would take 2 days. What transportation option do you suggest and what are the cost implications?

## Scenario 16
**Category:** inventory_management
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** You need to shelve 15 boxes but the assigned place is already full. What do you do?

## Scenario 17
**Category:** inventory_management
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** The inventory management system alerts you that stock levels for a popular item are below the reorder point. What information do you need to check before creating a purchase order?

## Scenario 18
**Category:** receiving
**Difficulty:** advanced
**Situation:** A large shipment arrives during your shift but you notice the delivery note shows a different company name than expected. The driver insists the goods are correct. How do you handle this?

## Scenario 19
**Category:** storage
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** The warehouse receives a delivery of seasonal goods for Christmas in September. The warehouse is currently full with regular inventory. Where and how should these goods be stored?

## Scenario 20
**Category:** packing_shipping
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** An international shipment requires customs documentation including a packing list, commercial invoice, and certificate of origin. A colleague asks you to explain the purpose of each document.

## Scenario 21
**Category:** order_picking
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Situation:** During order picking you accidentally drop and damage a product. The item is needed for a customer order that ships today. What steps do you take?

## Scenario 22
**Category:** order_picking
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** Your company wants to make picking faster to have good customer service. The manager asks for your suggestions.

## Scenario 23
**Category:** traffic_management
**Difficulty:** advanced
**Situation:** A customer needs regular weekly deliveries of temperature-sensitive products. What should you take into account?

## Scenario 24
**Category:** receiving
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Situation:** A delivery arrives with goods on EUR-pallets but your warehouse uses industrial pallets. The forklift drivers say this causes handling problems. How do you address this with the supplier?

## Scenario 25
**Category:** packing_shipping
**Difficulty:** advanced
**Situation:** Your warehouse ships to both domestic and international customers. A trainee asks you who is responsible for the differences in packing and documentation requirements. What do you tell them?
